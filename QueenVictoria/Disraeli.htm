DISRAELI, Benjamin (1804-1881)

Son of the novelist Isaac D'Israeli, after overcoming his reputation as a popular novelist and his Jewish background, he emerged as a more serious politician by the 1840s. He became Prime Minister on Lord Derby's retirement in February 1868, he lost the subsequent election but won the one of 1874, when his Government passed a series of social reform, also his secretary lamented that he had no ideas or proposals at the beginning. When he died in 1881 Queen Victoria, with whom he had closely working relationship, sent bunches of primeroses to his funeral.
