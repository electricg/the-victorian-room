SONS AND DAUGHTERS

From the marriage of Victoria and Albert, the Queen had nine children: Victoria, Edward, Alice, Alfred, Helena, Louise, Arthur, Leopold and Beatrice.

See the entire family tree of the Royal Family

Edward became King with the name of Edward VII in 1901 and he married the Princess of Denmark Alexandra in 1863
Victoria, Queen's first daughter, married Frederick III KIng of Prussia and their son was William II of Prussia, the last Kaiser of the German Raich. His sister, Sophie, married Constantine of Greece, who was King twice: form 1913 to 1917 and from 1920 to 1923).
Alice's daughter, Alix, married Tsar Nicholas II of Russia and became tsarina with the name of Alicky. She and all her family were killed by the Soviet in 1918. Anastasia, the protagonist of the legend, was her daughter.
Alfred, the fourth child, had a daughter, Marie, that became Queen of Romania after having married Ferdinand, who was King from 1914 to 1927. The same was for Arthur's daughter, who married Gustav VI of Sweden, becoming Queen and for Beatrice's daughter, Victoria, who married Alfonso XIII of Spain.

With these marriages the English Royal family became related to all the principal royal families of Europe.
