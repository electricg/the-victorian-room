GLADSTONE, William Ewart (1809-1898)

There has never been another Gladstone that still have ten more years in Parliament and two further terms vas Prime Minister to go. In 1859 he supported the Derby/Disraeli Reform Bill, but that year he finally gave his adherence to the Liberal Party, where he remained for the rest of his career. When Palmerstone died he became leader of the House of Commons and was responsible for introducing the Reform Bill of the 1866 into the Commons. He united all the constituent parts of the United Kingdom in a common cause against the Irish Church. As Prime Minister his Government was condemned for his approach to the reforms of the major institutions of the state, the army, the judiciary, the law and the universities and he first legally recognized the Trade Unions.
