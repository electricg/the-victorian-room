ENTERTAINMENT AT HOME

Making music at home was very popular with all but the very poorest classes. One of the most widespread forms of family entertainment in the Victorian period was an evening of songs and recitals around the piano. Amateur dramatics were another very popular way of passing the time at home, especially at holiday times such Christmas. Plays were performed, and often written, by different members of the family and home-made costumes, scenery and props were produced from whatever was available. Reading also passed the time. Novels were extremely popular in the nineteenth century. A method of cheap serial production was first used for the work of Charles Dickens and later for other famous authors such as the Bronte sisters.
