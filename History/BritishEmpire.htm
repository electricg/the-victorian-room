BRITISH EMPIRE

The British Empire took on enormous proportion, involving Canada, Australia, New Zealand; India, Ceylon, the Malay States, Singapore and Honk Kong in Asia; Gibraltar, Malta and Cyprus in the Mediterranean.

Britain was not the only country to have or wanted imperial control over other countries.In the nineteenth century France, Belgium and Germany were all seeking colonies and overseas defence outposts for the same reasons as Britain.

The eighteenth century saw huge competition between Britain and France for the control in North America, but then Britain lost hers in the American War of Independence. The nineteenth century saw the main theatre of conflict shift to Africa, with many of the major European nations attempting to establish control over the areas of territory that had their natural resources needed by their own industries.

Several territories were held in close contact with Britain because of a large number of Britain settlers who emigrated there to find a better life (or as convicts), for instance, in the new, barely known lands of Australia and New Zealand.

After 1875, England penetrated into Africa, obtaining the majority of shares in the control of the SUEZ CANAL, and extended rule over Uganda, Kenya, Rhodesia and the Niger territories. From 1899 to 1902 England was at war with the South African provinces of Orange and the Transval, where gold and diamonds had been discovered. These regions were ruled by Dutch colonists called Boers.The war lasted about 3 years; English public opinion was divided, as many considered this war unjust; it ended in 1902, after Victoria's death, giving England supremacy over the two Dutch colonies.
