THE VICTORIAN COMPROMISE

The Victorian Compromise saw prosperity and progress (invention of telephone, telegraph, photography, etc.) from one side and poverty, ugliness and injustice from the other; it opposed ethical conformism to corruption, moralism and philanthropy to money and capitalistic greediness, and it separated private life from public behaviour.
However, it also aroused the concern of more and more theorists and reformers who, for social and humanitarian reasons,tried to improve living conditions at all levels, including hospital, schools and prisons. It was mainly due to their "crusades" if, in this period, Parliament passed so many Acts regulating work, sanitation, the exploitation of little boys, the conditions of children in general.
